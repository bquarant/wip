Dear Brian,

As the new Editor-in-Chief of the Journal of the International Neuropsychological Society, I would like to personally thank you for your current and past contributions in reviewing manuscripts for the journal.  I sometimes ask myself why we take the time out of our busy schedules to provide detailed, constructive criticisms of other individuals work.  Aside from the altruistic reason that the peer review process is essential to ensure that only the best scientific work is published in the field of neuropsychology, I believe a major motivation is our interest in learning from our colleagues.  Reviewing manuscripts not only enables us to keep on top of emerging trends in the field, but it also sharpens our critical skills to improve our own work.

For whatever reason we conduct article reviews, I think we can all agree that the process is an educational one.  In this context, I am writing today to inform you of a new program being instituted at JINS.  In collaboration with the student organization of the International Neuropsychological Society, we have developed a mentoring program for graduate/undergraduate students and postdoctoral fellows interested in learning how to review scientific manuscripts.  In effect, we are trying to formalize and encourage the training of the next generation of article reviewers using a mentoring model.

Im sure that many of you have already reviewed manuscripts with your students.  Typically, however, journals only recognize the contributions of the primary reviewer and do not track or provide acknowledgements of student contributions to the review process.  When you now submit a review to JINS, you be given the opportunity to enter the name, institution, and email address of the student/fellow.  With the last issue of the calendar year, JINS will publish a list of student ad-hoc reviewers in conjunction with our listing of ad-hoc reviewers.  In this way, we hope to formalize the mentoring program that currently exists and to encourage mentors and students to partner in this important training experience.

Sincerely,

Stephen M. Rao, PhD
Editor-in-Chief, JINS
